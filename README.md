# Power-BI-Hospital-Pharmacy-Report
Most common diseases across counties are Flu, Typhoid, Diabetes and Hypertension.
High patient visits doesn't translate to high pharmacy revenues as it will depend of unit price of the drug commodities and reason for the visit. Follow up and wellness check visits have minimal pharmacy revenues.

Total patient visits are 319K while Pharmacy Revenue achieved is 275K.
In patient department generated the highest pharmacy cost at 95K
The Elderly age group  consume most of the medications than other age group categories -  39.91%

On whether certain diagnoses are associated with longer length of stay with minimal pharmacy revenue from the data it's not true.
Typhoid, Pneumonia, Malaria and Hypertension are all associated with longer length of stay and good pharmacy revenues.
The data demonstrates trends of certain conditions across counties that will inform the need for Hospital In reach programs and Community Outreaches to help identify clients at risk and role out more health education and advocacy.





Certain diagnosis contribute to higher patient visits while resulting to lower Total Costs like Flu while malaria recorded the lowest patient visits with a higher Total Costs.
